Understanding the Correlation Coefficient

The correlation coefficient (r = 0.89) quantifies the strength of the relationship between algal blooms detected by Sentinel satellites and microplastic concentrations measured by EMODnet.

This high correlation enables us to accurately predict microplastic hotspots using satellite data where direct measurements are unavailable.

Strong Positive Correlation

Data Sources

Our platform integrates multiple data sources for comprehensive microplastic tracking

EMODnet

European Marine Observation and Data Network providing ground-truth microplastic measurements from thousands of sampling stations.

Sentinel-2

High-resolution multispectral imaging for algal bloom detection using NDCI (Normalized Difference Chlorophyll Index).

Landsat

Long-term Earth observation data for historical pattern analysis and validation of correlation models.

Copernicus

European Union's Earth observation program providing comprehensive environmental monitoring data.
